# Vertifile Testimonial & Case Study Interview Guide

**Purpose:** Capture authentic customer stories for marketing videos, website testimonials, and case studies.
**Interview Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Final Edit Target:** 2-3 minutes per testimonial

---

## 10 Interview Questions

Ask in this order. Let the interviewee talk -- the best moments come from follow-up, not the scripted question.

### Background & Problem

1. **Tell us about your role and your organization.**
   *Goal: Establish credibility and context. Get their title, company, and what they do day-to-day.*

2. **Before Vertifile, how did you verify that documents were authentic?**
   *Goal: Surface the old, painful workflow. Manual checks, phone calls, hoping for the best.*

3. **Can you describe a specific incident where a forged or tampered document caused a problem?**
   *Goal: Get a concrete story. Names can be changed, but the situation should be real and specific.*

4. **What was the cost of that problem -- in time, money, or reputation?**
   *Goal: Quantify the pain. Even rough numbers are powerful on camera.*

### Discovery & Adoption

5. **How did you first hear about Vertifile, and what made you decide to try it?**
   *Goal: Understand the discovery channel and the trigger that moved them from awareness to action.*

6. **What was the onboarding experience like? How long before you were up and running?**
   *Goal: Demonstrate ease of adoption. Fast setup is a selling point.*

### Results & Impact

7. **Walk us through how you use Vertifile in your daily workflow now.**
   *Goal: Show practical, real-world usage. This becomes the "how it works in practice" footage.*

8. **What specific results have you seen since adopting Vertifile?**
   *Goal: Get measurable outcomes -- time saved, fraud caught, trust increased, compliance met. Push for numbers.*

9. **Has Vertifile changed how your clients, partners, or stakeholders perceive your organization?**
   *Goal: Capture the trust and reputation angle. This is often the most emotionally compelling answer.*

### Recommendation

10. **If someone in your industry was skeptical about document verification, what would you tell them?**
    *Goal: Peer-to-peer recommendation. This is the money quote -- the one that goes on the website.*

## What to Capture on Camera

### Interview Setup
- Interviewee seated in their actual workspace (not a studio -- authenticity matters)
- Clean background, natural light preferred, ring light as backup
- Frame: Medium close-up, rule of thirds, looking slightly off-camera at the interviewer
- Two camera angles if possible: main (close-up) and wide (shows environment)
- Lavalier mic on interviewee, shotgun mic as backup
- Record in 4K if available, deliver in 1080p

### Must-Have Shots
- Interviewee speaking naturally (not reading)
- Genuine reactions -- laughing, thinking, nodding
- Hands gesturing while explaining a process
- Interviewee at their computer showing Vertifile in their actual workflow
- Close-up of their face during the most impactful answer (usually Q3 or Q10)

### Screen Capture
- Record their screen separately while they demo their workflow (Q7)
- Capture the green stamp verification moment from their perspective
- Show the actual documents they work with (redact sensitive content in post)

## B-Roll Suggestions

Capture 30-60 seconds of each. These are used as cutaways during editing to cover jump cuts and add visual variety.

### At the Interviewee's Location
- Exterior shot of their office building / campus
- Team members working at desks
- Interviewee walking through the office or greeting a colleague
- Documents being printed, signed, or filed
- Interviewee typing / using their computer (over-the-shoulder angle)
- Close-up of hands on keyboard
- Close-up of documents on a desk

### Product-Specific B-Roll
- Screen recording: uploading a PDF to vertifile.com
- Screen recording: PVF file being opened, stamp turning green
- Screen recording: tampered file being detected, stamp turning red
- Mobile device showing Vertifile in a browser
- Email with a PVF attachment being opened

### Generic / Supplementary
- Time-lapse of the office through a workday
- Coffee cup on desk next to a computer (establishing shot)
- Close-up of a printed certificate or diploma
- Hands exchanging documents across a table
- Server room or IT infrastructure (if relevant to the customer's industry)

## Pre-Interview Checklist

- [ ] Send questions to interviewee 48 hours in advance (let them prepare, not memorize)
- [ ] Confirm location, lighting, and background in advance (do a video scout if remote)
- [ ] Get signed release form before recording
- [ ] Test all audio and video equipment 30 minutes before start
- [ ] Have backup batteries and storage cards
- [ ] Prepare a one-page brief on the interviewee's company and role
- [ ] Bring water for the interviewee
- [ ] Plan for 45 min recording to get 2-3 min of usable content

## Post-Interview

- [ ] Label all footage immediately (name, date, question number)
- [ ] Back up to two separate drives
- [ ] Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Share rough cut with interviewee for approval before publishing
- [ ] Extract 2-3 pull quotes for social media and website
