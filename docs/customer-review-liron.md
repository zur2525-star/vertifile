# Customer Review — Liron (Head of Ops, Israeli Law Firm, ~80 lawyers)

*Reviewed 2026-04-09, approximately 30 minutes spent. I was sent here because opposing counsel on two recent matters claimed our filings had been altered after the fact, and I have no forensic tool to disprove that quickly. If Vertifile does what it says, this is a problem worth paying for.*

---

## Profile

I run operations at a mid-size litigation and commercial firm in Tel Aviv. ~80 lawyers, heavy document flow, iManage DMS. I am not a cryptographer — I buy tools on behalf of partners who will ask me exactly two questions: "can I trust it in court?" and "how much does it cost per lawyer per year?" I have been pitched 30 SaaS products this year and 25 of them were vapor.

---

## First Impressions (60 seconds)

- The landing page headline `Verify & Protect Any Document / Tamper-Proof in Seconds` (`public/index.html:686`) is clear enough that I understood the product in 4 seconds. That almost never happens. One sentence to my managing partner: "A tool that freezes a red stamp on a document the moment anyone touches it after you send it." Good.
- The hero sub-line `Issue tamper-proof documents with live holographic stamps. Let anyone verify them instantly — no software needed.` (`public/index.html:687`) is the only reason I kept reading. The "no software needed" part is the purchase unblock for us — our opponents and the courts are not going to install a Vertifile app.
- The hero doc animation uses a **TEL AVIV UNIVERSITY — Certificate of Degree** example (`public/index.html:706-711`). As an Israeli reader this lands well. As a lawyer reader I immediately wondered: why am I not seeing a **contract** or a **court filing** as the hero example? That's my industry. Small thing, big signal.
- The stats strip — `bit-256 SHA-256 Encryption / +10k Documents Protected / 5 min Token Refresh / 8 Security Layers` (`public/index.html:761-764`) — made me raise an eyebrow. "bit-256" is not a thing. "SHA-256" is a hash function, not an encryption algorithm. If a partner who knows anything about crypto reads that, my credibility for recommending this product dies on the spot. **Please fix this before anyone from a security team sees the homepage.**
- The Trust section (`public/index.html:857-890`) gave me exactly two real credentials: "Patent Filed" (Israel, not granted) and "IANA MIME type #1446680 pending." That is honest. It's also thin. I did not see a single customer logo, a single case study, a single audit, or a single partner name. For a product whose whole pitch is "trust us," the evidence that anyone else trusts you is missing.

## What I Loved

- **SECURITY.md is the best vendor security doc I have read this year.** (`SECURITY.md:15-27`) The "For your security team — 30 seconds" opener does the job — it tells my (outsourced) CISO exactly what to do: fetch the public key, run ten lines of openssl, done. Most vendors bury this behind NDAs. I would forward this document verbatim to our IT consultant with zero edits. The "What happens if Vertifile goes out of business?" answer in section 6 (`SECURITY.md:266-267`) was genuinely clever — it's the one question every GC asks and nobody answers honestly.
- **Three independent verification methods published with working code.** (`SECURITY.md:140-236`) openssl, Node, and Python examples all use the same concrete values from a real signed document. If my IT team can run three commands and get "Signature Verified Successfully" back, that is evidence I can put in front of a partner without waving my hands. This closes 80% of the technical due diligence.
- **The dual-signature architecture is explained in plain language.** (`SECURITY.md:43-73`) I am not a cryptographer. I still understood that HMAC is the fast internal check, Ed25519 is the "anyone in the world can verify it" check, and they cover the same payload so you cannot have one match while the other fails. The sentence `The verifying party needs no account with Vertifile, no API credentials, and no runtime dependency on us` (`SECURITY.md:84`) is exactly the property I need in a courtroom.
- **The pending-patent and IANA MIME claims are specific enough to verify.** (`public/index.html:870,877`) Patent office, IANA request #1446680 — I can look those up. Most startups write "patent pending" and hope. You gave me a tracking number. Respect.
- **The "Phase 2E refuses to issue HMAC-only documents" commitment.** (`SECURITY.md:244`) Silent downgrade is how most cryptosystems fail in the real world. Writing down "the platform physically refuses" — and saying "document creation fails with a loud error rather than producing a partially-signed file" — is the kind of thing I want to hear. It tells me you thought about adversarial cases, not just the happy path.

## What Made Me Hesitate

- **The FAQ contradicts the dashboard paywall.** `public/index.html:1166` says *"Yes, Vertifile is free for individuals. Enterprise plans are available for organizations that need bulk processing, API access, and custom branding."* But `public/app.html:1517-1522` shows a paywall modal that says **"Upgrade to Download & Share — Your document is protected, but downloading and sharing requires a Pro or Enterprise plan — Upgrade to Pro — $29/mo."** And `public/app.html:2192,2198,2206,2212` gate the download AND share functions behind `isFreePlan()`. So the "free" tier is effectively: upload → see that it's protected → pay $29 to actually do anything with it. **This is a bait-and-switch and it is the single fastest way I would lose trust in a product.** Pick one story. "Free" that doesn't let you download or share is not free, it's a demo. Say that on the homepage.
- **There is no pricing page linked from the hero or the top of the funnel that I can read in 10 seconds.** The nav has `/pricing` (`public/index.html:610`), and the Settings tab links to it (`public/app.html:1403`), but the homepage never actually states a price. I have to find the paywall inside the dashboard to learn it's $29/mo for Pro, and I still don't know what Enterprise costs, what the per-seat model looks like, whether there is an annual discount, or whether 80 lawyers times 80 matters per month fits in any of the tiers. My CFO would not let me budget this.
- **The "8 Security Layers" section is half-real, half-theater, and the theater hurts the real.** (`public/index.html:890-960`) Layers 1 and 2 — Cryptographic Signing and Blockchain Timestamp — are the real product. Layers 4, 5, 6, and 7 — Environment Detection, DevTools Detection, Right-Click Block, Print Prevention — are client-side JavaScript anti-inspection tricks that any moderately skilled forensics expert will laugh at. And I saw exactly that code in `templates/pvf.js:406-445` (setInterval debugger trick, window.outerWidth check) and `templates/pvf.js:462-475` (blocking F12 and Ctrl+Shift+I). A court forensics expert WILL open DevTools on a PVF to inspect the signature. When they do, `templates/pvf.js:468,472` calls `freezeStamp()` — meaning the stamp turns red the moment the honest expert tries to verify the document. **That is a feature that actively makes your product look guilty when a legitimate forensic examiner looks at it.** I would hide the "DevTools Detection" marketing bullet entirely before showing this to any legal expert.
- **Screen Recording Detection will fire on every legitimate use case that matters to me.** (`templates/pvf.js:501-540`) A PVF that goes dark the moment someone starts a Zoom screen share will embarrass us in a deposition, a remote court hearing, or a client walkthrough. The code in `templates/pvf.js:506-516` literally blurs the document and overlays `"Screen Recording Detected — This document cannot be captured."` — I would get an angry phone call from a senior partner within one day of rolling this out. This is a hostile-to-the-user "security" layer that makes the product unusable in the exact situations lawyers need it.
- **The context-menu and Ctrl+S block prevents a recipient from saving the document locally.** (`templates/pvf.js:462,470`) If opposing counsel sends me a PVF and I want to save a local copy to our iManage DMS, I can't. I have to re-download from a share link. That's going to break every document-retention workflow we have. It's also going to make me doubt the document even exists outside the vendor's servers — the opposite of the trust story SECURITY.md sells.
- **The hash in the document preview is truncated to 16 characters plus `...` with no "show full" option.** `public/app.html:2129` does `(selectedDoc.hash || '--').substring(0, 16) + '...'`. I'm a skeptic: when you hide 48 characters of a hash from me, I wonder what you're hiding. The Cryptographic Proof accordion at `public/app.html:1328-1330` shows Algorithm, Key ID, and a Status pill — but I cannot see the actual signature, I cannot copy the full hash, and I cannot compare it against the value in SECURITY.md. Your own security doc tells me to verify payloads myself; the dashboard then refuses to show me the payload. Please add a "show full hash" toggle and a "copy signature" button.
- **`public/app.html:2897` whitelists `zur2525@gmail.com` and `info@vertifile.com` in `isFreePlan()` so the admin always has full access.** A Gmail address hard-coded in the client-side paywall check is unprofessional — my CISO would flag this in a five-minute review. It's also trivial to notice if any curious customer opens the source. Move this server-side.
- **Right-click is globally blocked on the PVF viewer.** (`templates/pvf.js:462`) As a lawyer I sometimes right-click to inspect an image, save it as evidence, copy a link, or use my browser's built-in translate. Blocking right-click on the entire document makes the viewer feel like a 2005 DVD anti-piracy screen. It will not stop a determined attacker and it WILL annoy every recipient you send a document to.
- **The "Built in Israel" trust card is the only geography signal.** (`public/index.html:883-884`) For an Israeli law firm this is a plus. For a US or EU enterprise considering this for regulated documents, it raises a completely different question — "where are you hosting the data and what's the GDPR/data-residency story?" — that you do not answer on the page. If you're targeting the US legal market in addition to Israel, you need a neutral "hosted in EU / SOC 2 / HIPAA-ready" strip next to it.
- **The FAQ is too shallow.** (`public/index.html:1143-1180`) Six questions, and none of them are the questions I actually have: "Is a Vertifile signature admissible in an Israeli court?", "What law firms use this today?", "Do you integrate with iManage or NetDocuments?", "What happens if I want to revoke a document I issued by mistake?", "Can I use this on a contract that also has DocuSign wet signatures?", "Does the stamp embed in the PDF or is the PVF a completely different file format my recipients have to learn?" Every single one of those is on my list. None are answered.
- **The language picker in `public/app.html:1071-1081` supports 10 languages, but I saw exactly one data-i18n attribute on the paywall copy at `1517-1522` and one on the settings plan copy. What happens when a Hebrew user hits the paywall? Does the modal localize?** I couldn't tell from a code read — which means there's a 50% chance a Hebrew user sees English strings in the middle of a Hebrew-localized dashboard. For Israeli market targeting, that matters more than most.

## What I'd Need Before Buying

- **SOC 2 Type II attestation** — or at minimum a published "in progress with [auditor] targeting [date]" statement. My cyber-insurance renewal questionnaire asks for it, my DMS vendor has it, and I'm not going to make Vertifile the one exception.
- **A real case study or customer logo in the legal or financial sector.** One named firm, one named credentialing body, one Fortune-anything logo. The industries strip at `public/index.html:1066-1096` lists "Legal — Contracts, court documents, notarizations" as a use case, but I don't see a single named customer from that industry. Give me a law firm that has used this in a real matter, and I will forgive a lot of the other issues on this page.
- **Integration with iManage or NetDocuments.** I file every document into a DMS. If Vertifile can't push a PVF into iManage as a native version, I have two systems of record and my partners will not tolerate that. A published Zapier / webhook / REST endpoint documented for DMS ingestion would get me moving.
- **An Israeli court precedent or rabbinical opinion confirming a Vertifile signature is admissible as evidence.** Even one. Your own SECURITY.md section 5 (`SECURITY.md:255`) honestly says *"We describe the mechanism; we do not opine on its legal effect."* Respect, but I still need the opinion to hand to my partner. Commission it.
- **Clear, public pricing with per-seat/per-document/per-firm math — and a resolution of the "free vs. paywall" conflict described above.** I cannot put a procurement request in front of my managing partner that says "the founder's Gmail is hardcoded in the JS."

## Would I Recommend This in My Partner Meeting Tomorrow?

**No — not yet. But closer than most things I've seen this year.**

Here is the honest picture. The **cryptographic core** of Vertifile is the best I have seen in the document-authenticity space. SECURITY.md is genuinely good. The dual-signature model is understandable, verifiable, and truly independent of Vertifile as a company — meaning my firm can trust a document even if Vertifile dies tomorrow. That is a rare and valuable property. If the product I saw today were *just* that core — SECURITY.md, the verify endpoint, and a clean upload/download flow — I would recommend a paid pilot on the spot.

What I cannot recommend is the **envelope around the core**. The homepage says "free" while the dashboard paywalls downloading and sharing. The "8 security layers" pitch mixes legitimate cryptography with DevTools-blocking and screen-recording-detection client tricks that will either embarrass us in real use or actively insult any forensic expert we pay to verify a document. The truncated hash in the sidebar, the right-click block, the hostile keyboard shortcuts, and the "this document cannot be captured" overlay make the viewer *feel* like a product that is hiding something — which completely undermines the "we publish our public key, verify it yourself" story in SECURITY.md. The two halves of the product are telling opposite stories to the user.

Fix the paywall contradiction, strip the client-side anti-inspection theater from the viewer (or move it to an opt-in "paranoid mode"), expose the full hash and signature in the dashboard, publish pricing, and land one named legal-industry customer. With those five things, I would happily put this in front of my managing partner and pay for a 12-month pilot for 10 lawyers. Without them, I'd be putting my professional credibility behind a tool whose cover story doesn't match its cryptography, and I can't do that.

## P.S. — One Thing That Confused Me

The moment I said "wait, what?" was reading `templates/pvf.js:468` and `templates/pvf.js:501-540` back-to-back. On the one hand, SECURITY.md (`SECURITY.md:17`) sells me on the idea that *"Vertifile is the only document authenticity platform where you don't have to trust us — you can verify every document we issue using only a 64-byte public key and ten lines of code."* On the other hand, the recipient-facing viewer refuses to open DevTools, refuses to let me right-click, refuses to let me save a local copy, and **blurs the entire document if my operating system so much as considers a screen-share API call**. Those two design choices cannot coexist in the same product. Either you want me to verify things myself using openssl (in which case, let me inspect the document in my browser), or you want to treat me like a pirate who must be prevented from looking at the bytes (in which case, stop telling me I can verify it myself). Right now Vertifile is doing both, and the contradiction is the biggest single reason I wouldn't walk into my partner meeting tomorrow with this product in hand.

Pick a lane. The verify-it-yourself lane is the one that wins.

— Liron
