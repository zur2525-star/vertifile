## How to Use These Templates

Each template below is a reusable structure for a common post type. Replace the bracketed `[PLACEHOLDER]` text with your specific content. Every template includes versions for Twitter/X (short) and LinkedIn (long), plus guidance on the visual asset and hashtags.

## Template 1: Stat Post

Use when: sharing a surprising statistic that highlights the document fraud problem or Vertifile's impact.

### Visual

Large number or statistic on a clean dark background (`#0f0e17`). Number in Inter 800, oversized (120px+). Source citation in small text below. Vertifile logo in corner.

No busy backgrounds. The number IS the visual.

### Twitter/X

```
[STAT NUMBER OR PERCENTAGE].

[ONE-SENTENCE CONTEXT — what does this stat mean?]

[ONE-SENTENCE IMPLICATION — why should the reader care?]

[OPTIONAL: One sentence connecting to Vertifile's solution]

Source: [AUTHOR/ORG, YEAR]

#Vertifile #DocumentSecurity [INDUSTRY HASHTAG]
```

**Example:**

```
$600 billion.

That's how much document fraud costs the global economy annually.

Most of it goes undetected because there's no efficient way to verify
documents at scale.

Source: International Chamber of Commerce, 2024

#Vertifile #DocumentSecurity #FraudPrevention
```

### LinkedIn

```
Here's a number that should concern every [TARGET AUDIENCE]:

[STAT — bold, standalone paragraph]

[2-3 sentences explaining the context. What does this stat come from?
What does it measure? Why is it growing?]

[2-3 sentences explaining the implication. What does this mean for
the reader's industry? What are the consequences of inaction?]

[1-2 sentences connecting to Vertifile. How does our technology
address this specific problem? Keep it factual, not salesy.]

Source: [FULL CITATION — author, publication, year, link if available]

#Vertifile #DocumentSecurity [3-4 RELEVANT HASHTAGS]
```

### Rules for Stat Posts

1. Every number must have a real, verifiable source. No fabricated statistics.
2. Source name, year, and publishing organization are required.
3. The stat should be genuinely surprising or counterintuitive.
4. Connect the stat to document verification naturally -- don't force it.
5. Visual: one number, one background. Nothing else competes for attention.

## Template 2: Feature Highlight

Use when: explaining a specific Vertifile feature or technology component.

### Visual

Feature diagram or icon on brand background. Can be dark (`#0f0e17`) or gradient (`#4f46e5` to `#7c3aed`). Feature name as headline. Supporting visual (icon, diagram, or screenshot).

### Twitter/X

```
[FEATURE NAME]:

[2-3 short sentences explaining what it does in plain language.
No jargon. A non-technical person should understand.]

[ONE SENTENCE — why this matters to the user]

[OPTIONAL CTA]

#Vertifile #[FEATURE HASHTAG] #DocumentSecurity
```

**Example:**

```
BLIND Processing:

Vertifile never reads your documents. We compute a cryptographic
fingerprint without accessing the content inside.

Your medical records, contracts, and diplomas stay private — even from us.

Privacy by architecture, not by policy.

#Vertifile #BLINDProcessing #Privacy #DocumentSecurity
```

### LinkedIn

```
[OPENING QUESTION or PROBLEM STATEMENT — 1-2 sentences that
identify the pain point this feature solves]

[FEATURE INTRODUCTION — "That's why we built [FEATURE NAME]."
or "Here's how [FEATURE NAME] works:"]

[HOW IT WORKS — 3-5 sentences explaining the mechanism.
Use analogies when helpful. Stay in business language, not
engineering language.]

[WHY IT MATTERS — 2-3 sentences connecting to business outcomes.
Compliance, risk reduction, efficiency, trust.]

[OPTIONAL: Comparison to how others do it — factual, not
disparaging]

[CTA — try it, learn more, or follow for next feature]

#Vertifile #[FEATURE HASHTAG] #DocumentSecurity [2-3 MORE]
```

### Rules for Feature Highlights

1. Lead with the problem, not the feature name.
2. Explain in business language. "Cryptographic fingerprint" is OK; "HMAC-SHA256 with server-side key rotation" is not.
3. Every feature connects to a user benefit. No features in isolation.
4. One feature per post. Do not bundle.

## Template 3: Comparison Post

Use when: contrasting Vertifile with existing approaches (PDF signatures, QR codes, blockchain certificates).

### Visual

Split image or comparison table. Before/After format (see Asset 2 in Visual Specs) or side-by-side feature grid. Red/green color coding for missing/present features.

### Twitter/X

```
[EXISTING APPROACH]: [what it does].
[VERTIFILE/PVF]: [what it does differently].

[EXISTING APPROACH]: [limitation 1].
[VERTIFILE/PVF]: [advantage 1].

[EXISTING APPROACH]: [limitation 2].
[VERTIFILE/PVF]: [advantage 2].

[CLOSING LINE — not disparaging, just factual]

#Vertifile #[COMPARISON HASHTAG]
```

**Example:**

```
PDF signature: proves who signed.
PVF: proves the document hasn't been altered.

PDF signature: static badge in file properties.
PVF: live animated stamp visible to anyone.

PDF signature: requires Adobe Acrobat.
PVF: works in any browser.

Not a replacement. A different layer of protection entirely.

#Vertifile #PDFvsPVF #DocumentVerification
```

### LinkedIn

```
Let's talk about what [EXISTING APPROACH] actually does — and doesn't do.

[2-3 sentences acknowledging what the existing approach does well.
Be respectful. These are real products that serve real purposes.]

Here's what [EXISTING APPROACH] was not designed to do:
- [Gap 1 — specific, factual]
- [Gap 2 — specific, factual]
- [Gap 3 — specific, factual]

Here's what Vertifile does differently:
- [Advantage 1 — directly addressing Gap 1]
- [Advantage 2 — directly addressing Gap 2]
- [Advantage 3 — directly addressing Gap 3]

[CLOSING — frame as complementary, not competitive.
"This isn't about replacing X. It's about adding a layer that
X was never designed to provide."]

#Vertifile #[COMPARISON HASHTAG] #DocumentVerification [2-3 MORE]
```

### Rules for Comparison Posts

1. Never disparage competitors by name. Compare approaches and categories.
2. Acknowledge what existing solutions do well before explaining gaps.
3. Be factually accurate. Every claim must be verifiable.
4. Frame as complementary ("adding a layer") not competitive ("replacing").
5. The Before/After visual does the heavy lifting. The text provides context.

## Template 4: CTA (Call to Action) Post

Use when: driving direct action -- signups, trial usage, demo requests.

### Visual

Product screenshot (upload interface or verification result) with brand overlay. Clear button or arrow pointing to the action. Minimal text on the image -- the post text carries the message.

### Twitter/X

```
[ACTION STATEMENT — imperative, short, clear]

[3-4 numbered steps showing how quick/easy it is]

[SINGLE QUALIFYING LINE — "No credit card." / "No signup." / "60 seconds."]

[URL]

#Vertifile #[ACTION HASHTAG]
```

**Example:**

```
Protect your first document in 60 seconds.

1. Go to vertifile.com
2. Upload any file
3. Get a tamper-proof .pvf with a live verification stamp

Free. No credit card. No signup required.

vertifile.com

#Vertifile #TryItFree
```

### LinkedIn

```
[1-2 sentences recapping the core value proposition —
what problem does Vertifile solve and why should the reader act?]

[TRANSITION — "Now it's time to see it yourself." or
"Words are good. Proof is better."]

[STEP-BY-STEP instructions — numbered, simple, specific.
Each step should take less than 15 seconds.]

[REMOVE FRICTION — explicitly state what's NOT required.
No credit card. No signup. No sales call. No software install.]

[FINAL LINE — direct, confident, not pushy.
"Just you, a document, and 60 seconds."]

[URL]

#Vertifile #[ACTION HASHTAG] #DocumentVerification
```

### Rules for CTA Posts

1. One action per post. Do not ask the reader to do multiple things.
2. Remove friction explicitly. Tell them what they DON'T need to do.
3. Be specific about time. "60 seconds" beats "quickly."
4. Include the URL. Always. Even on platforms where it's not clickable.
5. Never use "click the link in our bio" as the primary CTA on platforms where URLs work.

## Template 5: Industry Pain Point

Use when: addressing a specific industry's document verification challenges.

### Visual

Industry card from Asset 4 set (see Visual Specs). Icon + industry name + value prop on dark background. Alternatively: industry-specific document example with Vertifile stamp.

### Twitter/X

```
[INDUSTRY] problem:
[2-3 sentences describing a specific, real pain point.
Be concrete. Name the document types. Name the workflow.]

[1-2 sentences showing how PVF addresses this specific problem.]

#[INDUSTRY HASHTAG] #Vertifile #DocumentSecurity
```

**Example:**

```
Healthcare problem:
A patient's medical records travel between hospitals, labs,
insurers, and pharmacies. At every handoff, there's a risk of
alteration — and the consequences are clinical, not just administrative.

PVF-protected records carry a live stamp that instantly reveals
any modification. BLIND processing means even Vertifile never
reads the content.

#HealthTech #Vertifile #PatientSafety #HIPAA
```

### LinkedIn

```
[INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC OPENING — describe a real scenario.
Be specific enough that someone in this industry recognizes
their own workflow.]

[THE PROBLEM — 2-3 sentences explaining what goes wrong.
What's the gap in current verification? What are the consequences?
Use concrete examples: "a backdated contract," "an altered lab result."]

[THE IMPACT — 1-2 sentences on business/regulatory/safety impact.
Connect to compliance frameworks where relevant (HIPAA, SOX, etc.)]

[THE SOLUTION — 3-4 sentences explaining how Vertifile addresses
this specific workflow. Don't repeat the general product pitch.
Tailor it to the industry: "For hospitals, this means..." /
"For law firms, this means..."]

[CTA — industry-specific page or general trial]

#Vertifile #[INDUSTRY HASHTAG] #DocumentVerification [2-3 MORE]
```

### Rules for Industry Pain Points

1. Know the industry. Don't write generic posts with an industry label slapped on.
2. Name specific document types (diplomas, contracts, lab results, audit reports).
3. Name specific workflows (hospital-to-insurer transfer, candidate verification).
4. Connect to real compliance frameworks (HIPAA, GDPR, SOX, FERPA).
5. End with an industry-specific CTA, not a generic "try it free."

### Platform-Specific Notes

- **LinkedIn:** Max 5-7 hashtags. Place at end of post, after a line break.
- **Instagram:** Up to 15 hashtags. Mix of large and niche. Place in first comment or at end of caption.
- **Facebook:** Max 2-3 hashtags. Less is more on Facebook. Place at end of post.

## Tone and Voice Checklist

Before publishing any post, Lior confirms:

- [ ] Sounds like a confident professional, not a pushy salesperson
- [ ] No superlatives without proof ("best", "most secure", "only" -- must be factually true)
- [ ] Every statistic has a named, dated source
- [ ] Technical concepts explained in business language
- [ ] Competitor mentions are respectful and factual (or omitted)
- [ ] CTA is clear, specific, and includes a URL where applicable
- [ ] Post text and visual asset tell the same story (not two different messages)
- [ ] No decorative emojis -- clean text only
- [ ] Proofread for spelling and grammar
- [ ] Hashtags are relevant and correctly spelled
