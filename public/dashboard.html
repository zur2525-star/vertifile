Help & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if someone alters a protected document?▼
If a document has been altered after Vertifile protection was applied, the verification will fail immediately. The holographic stamp freezes red and displays a "FORGED" warning, alerting anyone viewing the document that it has been tampered with.

What is a PVF file?▼
A PVF (Protected Verification File) is Vertifile's proprietary format that bundles your original document with cryptographic verification data, digital signature, and holographic stamp. Opening it with the Vertifile viewer automatically verifies integrity in real time.
Was this helpful? Thanks for your feedback!
How does Vertifile protect my documents?▼
Vertifile creates a unique cryptographic hash of your document and embeds it with a live holographic verification stamp containing blockchain-backed digital signatures that can be verified by anyone, anywhere.
Was this helpful? Thanks for your feedback!
What file types are supported?▼
Vertifile supports PDF, images (PNG, JPG, TIFF), text files (TXT), and Word documents (DOCX). Maximum file size is 50MB per upload.
Was this helpful? Thanks for your feedback!
How do I share a protected document?▼
From the Documents tab, click the three-dot menu on any document and select "Copy Share Link." Anyone with the link can view and verify the document. Track link opens from your dashboard.
Was this helpful? Thanks for your feedback!
How do I upgrade my plan?▼
Go to Settings, then Plan & Billing. Click "Change Plan" to see available plans. Upgrades take effect immediately with prorated billing.
Was this helpful? Thanks for your feedback!
Can I customize my verification stamp?▼
Yes! Navigate to "My Stamp" to customize accent color, holographic wave colors, upload your logo, and choose stamp size. Changes apply to future protected documents.
Was this helpful? Thanks for your feedback!
Is my data stored securely?▼
All documents are encrypted at rest with AES-256 and transmitted over TLS 1.3. We follow SOC 2 Type II compliance. Your data is stored in geographically redundant data centers, and we never access document contents.
