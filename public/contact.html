FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Vertifile.

What is a PVF file?

A PVF (Protected Vertifile) is a tamper-proof document format that embeds a cryptographic signature and a live animated holographic stamp. It opens in any browser and can be verified by anyone without special software or an account.

Is Vertifile free to use?

You can try Vertifile with our free demo that allows up to 5 documents per hour. For unlimited access, API integration, and enterprise features, contact us for custom pricing based on your organization's needs.

Do you have an API?

Yes. Vertifile provides a RESTful API with SDKs for Node.js, Python, Java, and Go. You can protect and verify documents programmatically, set up webhooks, and integrate with your existing systems. Visit our developer documentation for details.

Can you see the content of my documents?

No. Vertifile uses BLIND processing, which means we never read, access, or store the content of your documents. Only a cryptographic hash (fingerprint) is computed on your device and sent to our servers. It is mathematically impossible to reconstruct a document from its hash.

How does blockchain verification work?

When a document is protected, its cryptographic hash is registered on the Polygon blockchain, creating an immutable, publicly auditable record. During verification, the hash is compared against the blockchain record. Any tampering is instantly detected because the hash will no longer match.
